Topic Of The Week


Magnus Carlsen Conquers the Inaugural Esports World Cup (Chess)

World No. 1 Magnus Carlsen claimed victory in the first-ever chess event at the 2025 Esports World Cup in Riyadh, defeating Alireza Firouzja in the Grand Final with back-to-back 3–1 wins.
The triumph earned him $250,000 from a $1.5 million prize pool.
The fast-paced 10-minute format thrilled 30,000+ live viewers and showcased Carlsen’s skill and sportsmanship.
Carlsen called it “new ground conquered,” marking a major step for chess in the esports arena.
